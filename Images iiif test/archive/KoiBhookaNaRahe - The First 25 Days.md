In the face of the COVID-19 pandemic, an alliance of volunteer groups and NGOs in Bidar, including the Humanitarian Relief Society, Reshine Organisation, Jamaat-e-Islami Hind (JIH), Bidar Revolution, Team YUVAA, Jilla Samagra, and Abhivrudhi Horata Samithi, stepped up to provide aid to those in need.

Beginning their efforts in earnest, they pooled resources and started distributing packed food, water bottles, and essential supplies to the poor and needy. By Day 6, they had distributed 310 food packets and water bottles, with the grateful recipients bestowing their heartfelt blessings upon the volunteers. Following the recommendations of food safety officials, they formalized their efforts under the banner of #KoiBhookaNaRahe (May No One Go Hungry), establishing the CovidWarriors (CoronaSainikaru).

Over the course of the following week, Team YUVAA's operations continued to expand. On Day 7, with increased discipline and more people coming forward to help, they distributed food at the Government Hospital premises in Bidar. Thanks to the support of the Humanitarian Relief Society and the Reshine Organisation, they were also able to distribute ration kits at Mailur by Day 8.

The increasing urgency of their mission became clear on Day 9 when an increase in positive cases led to stricter movement restrictions. Despite this, Team YUVAA managed to distribute food and made necessary adjustments to ensure no one went without.

The days rolled on with their efforts remaining steadfast. On Day 13, they provided food at BRIMS hospital for quarantined family members and the needy outside the hospital premises. Day 14 saw Team YUVAA distributing food in Mailoor, and by Day 15, their operations were in full swing across multiple locations.

As they crossed the half-month mark, the CoVIDwarriors continued their work undeterred. Food was distributed daily, and supplies were regularly sent out. Facebook posts and pictures from these days show the tireless dedication of the team.

Within the first 30 days of the lockdown - Team YUVAA with the coalition had successfully implemented a system of distribution that ensured that aid reached those in need.

## Day 8 of the food distribution at Government Hospital, Bidar.

One positive case is all it took to scare the hell out of us. It was being followed up since yesterday, and today morning things started to look really bad. Authorities became really strict and started to restrict all kinds of movement.

They disallowed our passes too till further notice. And we started to think how to keep this going as the need is only getting bigger. But fortunately, the test report of the sample sent yesterday came out negative and things calmed down. But still a worrying sign and indication of things to come.

We finished today's distribution.

For the first time, we fell short as there were too many people who were waiting for the food. We made other adjustments later in the night for them all. We just pray it doesn't come to our places, with prayers for the entire world too that this passes us all for the good. Thank you for keeping the support and encouragement coming.
